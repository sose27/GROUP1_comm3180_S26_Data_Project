# How Blue is California?

### Group Project by Noah Sebhatleab, Nina Lawson, and Sose Hovannisian

---

## Project Overview

California is widely known as one of the most Democratic states in the country. But how politically uniform is the state beneath its statewide reputation?

Our project explores county-level political behavior across California to better understand whether the “solid blue state” narrative reflects reality. By analyzing historical election results, 2024 proposition voting, and county demographic data, we investigate how geography, race, age, income, education, industry, and employment help explain why some counties vote strongly Democratic, strongly Republican, or shift depending on the issue.

We aim to show that California politics are more complex, regional, and multidimensional than statewide results alone suggest.

---

## Research Questions

- Is California politically uniform at the county level?
- Which counties consistently vote Democratic or Republican?
- Which counties are competitive or shifting over time?
- How do counties vote differently on social, economic, and criminal justice issues?
- What demographic and economic factors best explain county voting patterns?

---

## Datasets Used

### Historical Election Data
County-level presidential and statewide election returns from 2000–2024 used to analyze long-term partisan trends, voting margins, and county competitiveness.

### Proposition Voting Data
2024 California proposition results for all 58 counties. Used to compare county preferences across different policy areas.

Focus propositions:
- **Prop 3** – Constitutional right to marriage  
- **Prop 32** – Raise minimum wage  
- **Prop 36** – Tougher drug and theft penalties  

### County Demographic Data
County-level American Community Survey and census data including:

- Median age  
- Race / ethnicity composition  
- Income  
- Education  
- Industry sectors  
- Employment patterns

## Preliminary Findings

- California is blue statewide, but politically diverse locally.
- Coastal urban counties vote strongly Democratic.
- Inland and rural counties lean Republican.
- Counties often vote differently depending on the issue.
- Geography alone does not explain political behavior.
