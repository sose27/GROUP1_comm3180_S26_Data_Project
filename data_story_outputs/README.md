## Blog Post & Creative Output


Our project explores whether California is truly a uniformly “blue” state by analyzing county-level voting behavior, demographic trends, and proposition voting patterns. Rather than treating California as politically monolithic, we investigate how different counties respond to social, economic, and criminal justice issues. This folder contains the final written and interactive components of our project, *How Blue is California?*


---

## Blog Post

Our data blog presents the full narrative of our analysis in an accessible and engaging format. It combines visualizations, maps, demographic analysis, and proposition voting data to explain how political behavior varies across California counties.

Key components include:

- County-level presidential voting trends over time
- Demographic comparisons across counties
- Analysis of California proposition voting
- Geographic voting patterns and regional differences
- Discussion of how issue-specific voting complicates the “solid blue state” narrative

The blog is designed to stand alone and communicate our findings clearly without requiring readers to review the technical notebooks.

## Creative Output / Interactive Website

Our creative output is an interactive StoryMap website titled *If California Counties Could Talk...*

Link here: https://uploads.knightlab.com/storymapjs/eb6d6b010d008aa3afe162af271c60c8/how-blue-is-california/index.html 

The website personifies several California counties and allows users to explore how different regions politically “speak” based on their voting patterns, demographics, and proposition results.

Featured counties include:

- San Francisco County
- Los Angeles County
- Orange County
- Marin County
- Kern County
- San Joaquin County
- Shasta County

Through maps, county profiles, and narrative storytelling, the website highlights the political diversity that exists within California and demonstrates how counties respond differently depending on the issue being voted on.

The project combines data analysis with creative storytelling to make county-level political trends more engaging and easier to understand.
