## Our Data

### Data Sources
#### We combined multiple county-level datasets to analyze voting behavior across California from both political and demographic perspectives.

* American Community Survey (5 Year Estimates)
    * Provides detailed county-level demographic and socioeconomic data, including race, age, income, employment, industry, and education. This dataset allows us to understand the underlying characteristics that may influence voting patterns.

* MIT Election Data (Presidential Results by County)
    * Contains county-level presidential election results, which we use to establish baseline political leanings (e.g., Democratic vs. Republican tendencies) across counties.

* California Ballot Measure (Proposition) Data
    * Includes county-level voting results for statewide ballot propositions. Unlike candidate elections, this data captures how voters respond to specific policy issues, allowing us to isolate preferences on social, economic, and criminal justice topics.
